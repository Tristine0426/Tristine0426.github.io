    Overview

    Craigslist is an advertisement site for users to buy, sell, and trade locally at no cost to the sellers. It’s a platform to advertise household items, liquidated merchandise, lands, houses, and offer handy services, find job postings, explore local communities as well as participating in discussion forums. Since its launch in 1994, CraigList still holds its original design.

    PHASE 1: DISCOVERY & RESEARCH

    UNDERSTANDING THE USERS

    After reading through many reviews and customer’s opinions. There are 3 main reasons why some prefer to use Craigslist:


    Simple: The interface is simple enough to use even for those who are older and not familiar with the Internet.


    No Commitment: Users do what they need to do (buy, sell, trade) without needing an account. They don’t feel being lure or tempted to keep coming back to spend money. Where as e-commerce sites these days show options like “Today’s Deals”, “Based on Your Last Purchase” , “Things You May Like”, etc. This keep users scrolling and eventually buying more than they need to.


    Convenience: Whatever seller sells, they keep all of their profits without shipping cost.

    Now it is time to understand the user’s perspectives, I interviewed and came up with 3 potential users.

    “The options on the home page is overwhelming and confusing.”

    Chon N.

    “I have a busy lifestyle, so Craigslist allows me to shop for what I need for a good deal.”

    Takeaways

    The main complain for Craigslist post is the amount of spams, trust-worthy comes in second, and there were confusions over the options on the home page. I noticed these complains that create negativity torward Craigslist are due to how the users use the platform to advertise their contents. Craigslist currently only have 25 employees to maintain the site. Thus, my goal is to create cleaner porduct categories and interfaces to develop a more trust-worthy feel to the platform. In regard to spam issue, I will tackle this problem when designing the interface for the posting process when users can only post a certain amount of posts per day.

    COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

    Pros

    • Available as a mobile app, which is more portable and convenience
    • Free to use
    • Sellers can dispute negative reviews, which means there are support from the staff

    Cons

    • Dont support payment
    • Have to meet buyers in person to make sales
    • There are certain prohibited items such as live animals, firearms, perishable food, etc.
    • Letgo will generate a title for an ad post

    Pros

    • Available as a mobile app
    • Users can have their identity verified with their driver’s license so you know they’re trustworthy
    • Ads are displayed chronologically, but can sellers can "bump" the item back to the top for $2

    Cons

    • Cannot delete account, users can only deactivate it
    • Shipping fees may be included
    • Can only buy and sell locally

    Pros

    • More support from Facebook staff
    • More control of privacy
    • Intuitive and free of charge

    Cons

    • User information such as username and profile is exposed for as long as they has a Facebook account
    • Only available for Facebook users in the United States, United Kingdom, Australia and New Zealand
    • No buyer protection

    Based on the survey and the competitive analysis, whereas Craigslist has tremendous problems with spams and scam posts, its competitorss proved to have more control of the platform to counter this problem. For example, Letgo prohibit certain items and generate title for posts; Facebook and OfferUp provide much more customer support and provide user verification to establish trust.

    PHASE 3: VISUAL DESIGN

    WIREFRAMES: TURNING IDEAS INTO DESIGNS

    I created wireframes to connect the concepts, user flows, and branding together on paper, then digitized them using Figma.

    COLOR & STYLE GUIDE

    Colorwise, I went with the classic hue that represents compassion. Red and pink were my top choices. I went with pink because using red as primary color could be problematic. For example, red usually associated with an error.


    As for typeface, I picked Prompt. It is user-friendly and easy to see. Moreover, Google Application recently updated their typefaces. I noticed that it is rounder and has more latter space. Because TNRHumane is an Android application, using a similar typeface provide familiarity.

    WHAT I LEARNED

    Brainstorming in multiple sessions: The hardest part of this project was how to get started. The concept is fairly new and there were limited apps with this concept. I knew some user frustrations and goals from personal experience as a volunteer, I have some ideas and features I wanted to create but was overwhelmed when thinking about organizing the user flow. Ultimately, I did many brainstorming sessions, taking breaks in between to refresh my mind and repeat. This process helps me put my thoughts out in intervals, be able to analyze ideas, and branch out from those ideas.


    Sorting Competitors when there is none: Another challenge was finding a competitor to analyze. There was only one app developed in India that addresses the stray animals problems. None in the U.S. So I decided to pick my competitors based on the basic features I wanted to develop. For example, I wanted to have a social feature to connect with local volunteers and allow users to keep updates from fundraising events, training, low-cost clinics, etc. I studied FaceBook, Instagram, Pawpular, ASPCA, Twitter, LinkedIn. This helped me learn basic social media structures, what they all have in commons, why are they different? What are their differences? How their users interact with the platform?


    More options, more confusions: While conducting the 2nd usability test, I see that users will click on buttons to explore or find the shortest way to complete the task. This caused confusion as they did not know what they are doing, its purpose, and how they got to the screen they are at.
